When to start introducing solids?
Most pediatricians recommend introducing solid foods around 4-6 months when your baby shows these readiness signs:

Physical Signs:

Can sit up with minimal support
Has good head and neck control
Shows interest in food (reaches for your plate, watches you eat)
Can bring hands/objects to mouth
Developmental Signs:

The tongue-thrust reflex has diminished (they don't automatically push food out with their tongue)
Opens mouth when food approaches
Can turn head away when full
American Academy of Pediatrics Guidelines:

Not before 4 months - digestive system isn't ready
Around 6 months is ideal for most babies
Breast milk or formula remains primary nutrition until 12 months
Since you have a baby, you're likely approaching or in this window soon! The Omnilove Baby Fruit Feeder is designed for 4+ months and perfect for safely introducing first tastes of fruit, helping babies explore textures while preventing choking.

Always consult your pediatrician before starting solids to make sure your baby is ready!
