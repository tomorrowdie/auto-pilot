Cold brew ratios and timing
For cold brew with Peet's Single Origin Brazil Coffee, here are the optimal ratios and timing:

Cold Brew Ratios:

1:4 ratio (strong concentrate): 1 cup ground coffee to 4 cups cold water
1:6 ratio (medium strength): 1 cup ground coffee to 6 cups cold water
1:8 ratio (milder): 1 cup ground coffee to 8 cups cold water
Timing:

Steep time: 12-24 hours at room temperature or in refrigerator
Best results: 18-20 hours for optimal extraction
Storage: Concentrate keeps for up to 2 weeks refrigerated
Method:

Combine coarsely ground coffee with cold, filtered water
Stir gently to ensure all grounds are saturated
Cover and steep for 18-20 hours
Strain through fine mesh or cheesecloth
Dilute concentrate with water, milk, or ice as desired
Customers specifically mention this coffee works "very smooth" for cold brew, making it an excellent choice for this brewing method.

How to make it less acidic
Good news—the Peet's Single Origin Brazil Coffee is naturally low in acidity already! Customers consistently mention it's "not too acidic" and praise it for having "no acidic aftertaste." The Brazilian beans from Minas Gerais and the natural drying method create a smooth, naturally sweet coffee that's easier on the stomach than many other varieties.

To further reduce acidity:

Cold brew (18-20 hours) naturally produces lower acidity than hot brewing—this is your best option with this coffee
Add a pinch of baking soda (1/4 teaspoon per cup) to neutralize any remaining acid
Use filtered water at a lower temperature if brewing hot
Avoid over-extraction by using correct proportions (10g per 6 oz of water)
Add milk or cream, which binds to acids and reduces perceived acidity

Cold brew step-by-step guide
Here's a complete cold brew guide using the Peet's Single Origin Brazil Ground Coffee:

Equipment Needed:

Large jar or cold brew maker
Fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth
Cold, filtered water
Step-by-Step Process:

Measure: Use 1 cup coarsely ground coffee to 4-6 cups cold water (adjust ratio for strength preference)
Combine: Add coffee grounds to cold water in your container
Stir: Gently mix to ensure all grounds are fully saturated
Steep: Cover and refrigerate for 18-20 hours (12-24 hours range)
Strain: Filter through fine mesh or cheesecloth to remove all grounds
Store: Keep concentrate refrigerated for up to 2 weeks
Serving:

Dilute concentrate with equal parts water, milk, or serve over ice
Add cream, milk, or sweetener to taste
This Brazil coffee works particularly well for cold brew—customers specifically mention it produces a "very smooth" result with the natural dried fruit and hazelnut notes coming through beautifully without any bitterness.
